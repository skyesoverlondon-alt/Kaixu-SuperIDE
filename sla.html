2. Definitions

  • "Downtime" — a period of more than 5 consecutive minutes during which the kAIxU IDE and API are returning 5xx errors or are completely unreachable. Planned maintenance excluded.
  • "Monthly Uptime Percentage" — (Total minutes in month − Downtime minutes) / Total minutes in month × 100%
  • "Service Credit" — a credit applied to the next billing cycle, calculated as a percentage of the monthly fee for the affected calendar month
  • "Scheduled Maintenance" — maintenance notified at least 48 hours in advance via email and status page; excluded from Downtime calculation

3. Service Credits

If the Monthly Uptime Percentage falls below the committed level, the customer is entitled to a Service Credit as follows:

Monthly Uptime AchievedService Credit
99.0% – < 99.9% (Pro/Team) / 99.5% (Enterprise)10% of monthly fee
95.0% – < 99.0%25% of monthly fee
< 95.0%50% of monthly fee

Service Credits are the sole remedy for downtime events. Maximum total Service Credits in any calendar month shall not exceed 50% of that month's fee. Annual plan customers receive credits calculated on the monthly equivalent.

4. Credit Request Process

To claim a Service Credit:

  • Email billing@kaixu.app within 30 days of the end of the month in which the downtime occurred
  • Include: account email, affected date(s) and time(s) (UTC), and a description of the impact
  • We will verify against our monitoring logs and respond within 10 business days
  • Approved credits are applied to the next invoice; they are non-transferable and non-refundable as cash

6. Scheduled Maintenance

We aim to perform all maintenance outside of peak hours (typically 02:00–06:00 UTC on Sundays). Maintenance windows will be announced via:

  • Email to all organisation administrators at least 48 hours in advance
  • Status page update (linked from the IDE status bar)
  • Emergency maintenance (security patches) may begin with less notice; we will communicate as quickly as possible

7. Exclusions

This SLA does not apply to issues caused by:

  • Force majeure events (natural disasters, war, pandemic, etc.)
  • Actions or omissions by the customer or third parties outside our control
  • Customer-side network or hardware failures
  • Free plan accounts
  • Beta or pre-release features explicitly labelled as such
  • Scheduled maintenance windows
  • Third-party service provider outages (AWS, Cloudflare, Netlify) where we have no means of faster recovery; however, we will use our best efforts to restore service promptly
